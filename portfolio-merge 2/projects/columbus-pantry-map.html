Columbus PantryMap

An interactive map of ~90 mutual-aid food locations across Central Ohio — little free pantries, blessing boxes, and community fridges. Take what you need, leave what you can.

Open full-screen ↗ Source on GitHub ↗

Why

Inspired by the University of Washington's PantryMap pilot, which mapped Seattle's micropantry network and found it moves an estimated 4 million pounds of food a year. Columbus has a similar grassroots network, but it was scattered across three separate maps — this project brings them together.

How

Location data compiled from the Mini Pantry Movement map, the Columbus Blessing Boxes Project, and the Bexley Freedge, deduplicated across networks. Built as a single-file HTML app with Leaflet and OpenStreetMap — no backend, no build step.

Features

Category filters, name and neighborhood search, "near me" geolocation, and per-location popups with turn-by-turn directions and links to each source network.
